Legacy

Maison Heritage

Our legacy spans over a century of dedication to the finest traditions of European craftsmanship. From our founding in Paris to our global recognition, we remain committed to preserving the artistry of bygone eras.

1920
Founded in Montmartre by master cobbler Henri Dubois, establishing the first private atelier dedicated to vintage footwear preservation.
1955
Received Royal Warrant from European nobility, becoming the official custodian of historical footwear collections across three royal houses.
1978
Opened our London gallery, expanding our exclusive membership to include distinguished collectors from across the Commonwealth.
2020
Centennial celebration marked by the acquisition of Napoleon's personal riding boots, the crown jewel of our collection.

Haute Couture

Our restoration process combines time-honored techniques with modern preservation science. Each piece undergoes meticulous care by artisans trained in centuries-old European traditions.

1

Authentication & Appraisal

Our master craftsmen examine each piece using specialized techniques to verify authenticity, age, and historical significance through materials analysis and construction methods.

2

Archival Documentation

Comprehensive photography and detailed records are created, documenting the piece's condition, provenance, and unique characteristics for our private archives.

3

Preservation Treatment

Using traditional leather conditioning techniques and climate-controlled environments, we ensure each piece maintains its integrity for future generations.

4

Exclusive Presentation

Completed pieces are housed in custom-crafted display cases within our private viewing rooms, accessible only to verified collectors and members.

Master Artisan Workshop

Our workshop houses over 200 traditional tools, some dating back to the 18th century. Each restoration project is approached with the reverence of a museum conservator and the skill of a master craftsman.
